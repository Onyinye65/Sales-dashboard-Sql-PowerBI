# Summary of Findings
✅Sales Performance: Breaking Down $16.35M over $15.3M budget, finishing the year with $1.05 million ahead of target 

✅ Budget overperformance of $1.05M (+6.9%)
Sales consistently beat target from mid-year, peaking near $2M in December. 

✅ Bikes = 93.9% of all revenue
The Mountain-200 (Black & Silver variants) carried the entire portfolio at $0.92M–$0.97M each. Accessories and Clothing are nearly invisible at ~4% and ~2%.

✅ Top 10 customers clustered tightly between ($9.6K–$11.5K) with Jordan Turner at the top
No single customer dominates. Low concentration risk .

✅ North America and Europe dominate the sales map. Asia, Africa, and South America show very little activity.

# 💡 MY RECOMMENDATIONS

1. Diversify beyond bikes — a 94% single-category dependency is a strategic risk. Bundles and cross-sells on Accessories can lift margin without needing new customers.

2. Find out why sales exploded in the second half of the year.
Sales nearly doubled from mid-year onwards. Something caused that, either a promotion, a season, a product.

3. Reward best customers —The top buyers all spend roughly the same amount. There's no one spending significantly more than the others, which means there's no reason for customers to spend extra. Create a loyalty programme or VIP perks to encourage top customers to buy more.

4. Regional expansion — A push into Asia or South America could unlock a new revenue tier
